# Talking-points sheet — print this and tape it next to your camera

This is **NOT a script**. It's beats. Glance at it between takes, then
look at the lens and say it in your own words. Reading verbatim makes
you sound robotic. Improvising around these beats makes you sound real.

The first time you record, do five takes per act. By take 4-5 you'll
have internalized the structure and the words will come out naturally.
Pick the best take in editing. Throw the rest away.

---

## ACT 1 · The hook (0:00 – 0:35)

**Mood:** documentary, witness, slightly serious. Don't smile. Don't
gesture. Speak slowly.

**Pause for 3 full seconds before starting.** Look at the lens. Then go.

### The four beats you must hit

1. **Establish the scale.** "Last year, in my country, 9.6 million people lost money trading on the stock market."
2. **Cite SEBI.** "SEBI — our market regulator — published the numbers. 91% lost money. Average loss: ₹1.1 lakh. Total: ₹1,05,603 crore."
3. **Make it personal.** "I'm a developer in India. I know people who lost their tuition money chasing options."
4. **Name the product + mechanic.** "I built Finsight OS — a privacy-first AI guardian that puts a 12-second speed bump between traders and the next bad trade."

### Variations you can use (so you sound different on each take)

For beat 3 personalization, any of these works:

- "I know people who lost their tuition money chasing options."
- "A friend of mine in college spent two months of his fee money on Bank Nifty puts. He didn't tell anyone."
- "I have an uncle in a small town who borrowed from a relative to top up his trading account after his second margin call."
- "Almost everyone in my generation in India knows someone who's lost money this way."

Pick the one that's true for you. Authenticity reads on camera.

### Words to AVOID saying

- "Hi everyone" / "What's up guys" — you're not a YouTuber, you're a witness
- "Today I want to show you" — too commercial
- "Disrupt" / "leverage" / "synergy" — corporate jargon kills the documentary tone
- Sponsored-video speech patterns (rising inflection at end of sentences)

### Mic check before you roll

Say: "Testing one two, lakh, crore, Nifty, Gemma, behavioral guardian."
If "lakh" or "crore" sound mangled in playback, you have a mic problem,
not a pronunciation problem. Re-set the mic.

## ACT 2 · The voiceover (recorded after the screen recording)

You'll record this AFTER you have the screen-cap on a second monitor,
watching it play silently while you narrate live. The cursor pace
dictates your speaking pace.

### The five voiceover blocks

**Block A · Dashboard reveal (0:35 – 0:50)**

> "This is the dashboard. Real Nifty and Bank Nifty prices from Yahoo.
> Real paper trades persisted to a local SQLite database. The behavioral
> score on the right — eight ninety-two out of a thousand — comes from
> Gemma 4 running locally on my machine. There's no cloud."

Speak this in time with your cursor scrolling down the dashboard. Land
"There's no cloud." just as the cursor reaches the bottom.

**Block B · Setting up the trade (0:50 – 0:55)**

> "I've already lost on four trades today. The model says I'm in Revenge
> Trading. Watch what happens when I try to place another one."

Land "another one" exactly as your cursor hovers over the BUY button.

**Block C · Speed Bump fires (0:55 – 1:30) — THE EMOTIONAL CORE**

> "The button was enabled. Nothing was stopping me. But I had to type —
> letter by letter — what I was about to do. Fifteen words. Generated by
> Gemma in real time. Naming the specific pattern: Revenge Trading.
> Twelve seconds. That's all it takes for the rational part of the brain
> to come back online. The order still went through. I'm still in
> control. But the impulse path now has a speed bump in it."

Two pacing rules:
- "**Letter by letter**" should land exactly as you start typing on screen
- "**Twelve seconds**" should land exactly when the on-screen countdown hits 12

Practice this until it syncs. If you can't get it to sync, slow down or
speed up the screen recording in DaVinci by 5%.

**Block D · Technical guts montage (1:35 – 1:50)**

> "Real FIFO trade matching. Twelve API endpoints. Seven local engines —
> Gemma, ChromaDB SEBI grounding, paper trading, behavioral history,
> crisis protocol, multimodal vision, live broker integration. Everything
> runs on the user's machine."

Speak this in time with the cuts. One short sentence per cut. The list
of engines should land while the architecture diagram is on screen.

**Block E · Multilingual + crisis (1:50 – 2:20)**

> "It speaks four Indian languages. The same edge AI that protects an
> English-speaking engineer in Bangalore protects a Telugu-speaking
> shopkeeper in Warangal. When the model detects severe financial
> distress — above seventy out of a hundred — it suspends the Speed
> Bump and shows verified iCALL helpline numbers in the user's own
> language. And anyone with a Zerodha account can plug it into their
> real broker today, free, via the Live Kite Connect mode."

The "free" lands when the Live Kite pill comes into view.

## ACT 3 · The vision close (2:20 – 3:00)

Same setup as Act 1. Same lighting, same mic, same wardrobe. Identical
production quality.

**Pause for 2 seconds before starting.** This pause is the cut from
demo back to your face — let the audience reset.

### The three beats

1. **Restate the scale + audience.** "Nine point six million people. Seventy-two percent in small towns and rural India. Seventy-five percent earning under five lakh rupees a year. They run laptops just like this one — with patchy internet, in three or four languages."
2. **Name what Gemma 4 changed.** "Gemma 4 made the technology accessible. Finsight OS makes the protection inevitable."
3. **The closing line — say this exactly:** "Open source. MIT licensed. Free. Forever. Because behavioral guardianship shouldn't be a premium feature — it should be the default."

The closing line is the only verbatim sentence in the whole video.
Memorize it. Say it slowly. Look directly at the lens. Don't blink at
the end. Hold the gaze for one extra beat after "default" — that's the
shot the audience remembers.

### Things you can omit if you forget

If under time pressure you forget any of beats 1-2, that's fine. The
closing line is the non-negotiable one. Even a 30-second Act 3 that's
just "Open source. MIT licensed. Free. Forever. Because behavioral
guardianship shouldn't be a premium feature — it should be the default."
followed by a 5-second hold works.

## Post-take review questions

After each take, before doing another, ask yourself:

- Did I look at the lens (not the screen)?
- Did I speak slower than felt natural?
- Did I pause where the script asks me to pause?
- Was my voice level even (not getting quieter at the end of sentences)?
- If you reviewed playback: would a stranger find this convincing?

If 4 out of 5 of those are yes → keep this take, do one more for safety,
move on.

If 0-2 → reset, take a sip of water, do another. Don't try to "fix" a
bad take with another fast take while you're frustrated. Stand up, walk
around for 60 seconds.

---

## What "good" sounds like

Watch any 60-second clip from a Vox documentary on YouTube. That's the
register: confident, unhurried, lower-volume than commercial speech,
slight pauses between key phrases, no filler words.

You're not a YouTuber. You're a developer who built a thing because the
problem matters. Speak from there.
