# Finsight OS · Voiceover Brief & Narration Script

**Purpose:** 60-second cold open for the 3-minute Kaggle submission video. Three composite anonymized accounts of Indian retail F&O traders, voiced by a professional Indian-English narrator, set up the problem before the live Speed Bump demo.

**Ethical framing:** These are *composite* characters drawn from publicly available material. None is a real, identifiable person. The on-screen text says: *"Anonymized accounts based on SEBI investor-protection records and public retail-trader forums. Voiced by a professional narrator."* Don't claim these are real interviews.

---

## Source material attribution

The composites draw from three classes of public source material — none of which requires permission to reference:

1. **SEBI F&O Study FY2024-25** — public PDF on sebi.gov.in. Provides the aggregate statistics: 9.6 M traders, 91% losing, ₹1.1 lakh average loss per trader, ₹1,05,603 crore total, 75% earning under ₹5 lakh, 72% from B30 cities.

2. **Public retail-trader forums** — r/IndianStreetBets, r/IndiaInvestments, r/DalalStreetTalks on Reddit; #optionstrading and #fno hashtags on X/Twitter. These are posts traders made publicly about their own losses. We don't quote any specific post — we synthesize patterns.

3. **News reportage** — Mint, Moneycontrol, The Hindu Business Line have run multiple feature pieces on retail F&O losses since 2024. Quotes from these articles are public-domain factual reporting.

The composites combine the *patterns* visible across these sources. Specific figures (Rs. amounts, locations, occupations) are illustrative — chosen to match the demographic profile SEBI's study documents but not tied to any one individual.

## The 60-second narration script

Read time at typical documentary pace (~150 wpm): 60-65 seconds. Three voices, but the same narrator throughout — subtle variation in cadence between segments, no impressions or accent shifts.

### Open (0:00 – 0:08, 8 seconds)

> Last year, in India, **nine point six million people** lost money trading derivatives.
>
> *(short pause)*
>
> That's not a market correction. That's a public health crisis.

### Account 1 — Engineer in Bangalore (0:08 – 0:25, 17 seconds)

> Rohan is a software engineer in Bangalore. Twenty-eight years old, makes eleven lakh a year.
>
> Six months ago, after seeing a YouTube influencer turn ten thousand into a lakh, he started trading Nifty options. By April, he had lost three lakh sixty thousand rupees — most of it in the four hours after each losing trade, when he was *trying to win it back.*

### Account 2 — Student in Hyderabad (0:25 – 0:40, 15 seconds)

> Anjali is twenty-one. A B.Com student at a college in Hyderabad. Her father sends seven thousand rupees a month for tuition.
>
> She spent two months of that on Bank Nifty puts. *Just to see if she could be one of the nine percent.* She wasn't.

### Account 3 — Shopkeeper in Indore (0:40 – 0:54, 14 seconds)

> Vikram runs a small electronics shop in Indore. After his second margin call last June, he took a personal loan from his cousin to top up his trading account.
>
> He told no one. Not his wife. Not his accountant. Just kept clicking *Place Order.*

### Bridge to demo (0:54 – 1:00, 6 seconds)

> Every trading app in India is built to make Rohan, Anjali, and Vikram trade *more.*
>
> Finsight OS is built to give them — *one moment* — to think.

## Voice direction notes (read these to your narrator)

- **Tone:** Documentary, not dramatic. Think Krishna Pokharel narrating a WSJ piece, not a movie trailer.
- **Pace:** Unhurried. The pauses between accounts matter — ~1 second silence between each segment. The pause after "public health crisis" is the longest, ~1.5 seconds.
- **Emphasis:** Italics in the script mark where the narrator should drop volume slightly and slow down — those phrases are the emotional anchors. Don't shout them. Lean *closer* to the mic.
- **Pronunciation:**
  - *Nifty* — NIF-tee, not NIE-ftee
  - *Bank Nifty* — two even stresses
  - *Lakh* — like "luck" with a soft k, **not** "LACK" or "LAH-k"
  - *Crore* — KROH-r, single syllable
  - Indian first names — natural Indian-English pronunciation, not anglicized
- **Avoid:** false sympathy ("oh no, poor Rohan"), market-bro energy ("lost his shirt"), or news-anchor over-articulation.
- **The point:** these are real patterns, real people exist behind the composites. The narrator's job is to be a respectful witness, not a performer.

## Audio prep on receipt

Once you have the WAV:

1. **Listen once cold.** Mark any take you'd want re-recorded.
2. **Trim leading/trailing silence** to under 0.5 s.
3. **Normalize** to -3 dBFS peak in DaVinci Resolve / Audacity (don't compress; documentary-style benefits from natural dynamic range).
4. **Drop into the video** under a slow ambient bed — Pixabay has free "documentary ambient" tracks. Keep the music at -18 dBFS so the voice sits 15 dB above it.
5. **Add captions** (auto-generate in YouTube Studio, then proof-read pronunciation of "lakh" and "crore" — auto-captions tend to mangle these).
